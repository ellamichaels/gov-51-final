Legal Context - South Dakota

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23-1A-7 (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - Any offense. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23-1A-7 (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - An assault has occurred or attempt by physical menace has been made, and
        a violation of a protective order has occurred. [S.D. Codified Laws §
        23A-3-2.1(1),(2) (2013)]
      - An assault has occurred or attempt by physical menace has been made, and
        the person has assaulted their partner within the preceding 48 hours.
        [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-2.1(3) (2013)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23-1A-7 (2013)]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - Any law enforcement officer. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23-1A-7 (2013)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana is illegal. [S.D. Codified Laws § 22-42-6
        (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [S.D. Codified Laws §
        23A-3-27 (2013)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the arrest, if no accusatory instrument
        was filed. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(1) (2013)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the entire case has been dismissed.
        [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(2) (2013)]
      - The petitioner was acquitted at trial on all charges where expunction is
        sought. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(3) (2013)]
      - After successful completion of all conditions of a suspended imposition
        of sentence. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-27-17 (2013)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 10. [S.D. Codified Laws § 22-3-1(1) (2013)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - Eighteen, although an individual might remain under the jurisdiction of
        the juvenile court until the age of 21.. [S.D. Codified Laws §
        26-7A-1(3),(6) (2013)]

Cases Rejected for Prosecution

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [S.D. Codified Laws §
        23A-3-27 (2013)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the arrest, if no accusatory instrument
        was filed. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(1) (2013)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the entire case has been dismissed.
        [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(2) (2013)]
      - The petitioner was acquitted at trial on all charges where expunction is
        sought. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(3) (2013)]
      - After successful completion of all conditions of a suspended imposition
        of sentence. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-27-17 (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - Although not technically resulting in a conviction, a defendant who has
        successfully completed their probation term as part of a suspended
        imposition of sentence, the court shall then discharge and dismiss the
        matter without an adjudication of guilt, and thereafter order the
        sealing of all public records related to the matter.. [S.D. Codified
        Laws § 23-6-8.1 (2013)]
      - For non-public records maintained by the Bureau of Criminal Statistics,
        the director of the Bureau may authorize the destruction of records
        under the following circumstances: (i) the person has died; (ii) the
        person has attained the age of 75 years old and has not violated any
        laws in the previous 10 years; (iii) the incident involves offenses no
        longer considered crimes in the state; or (iv) misdemeanor offenses
        disposed of at least 10 years prior.. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-30
        (2013)]
      - The court has found by clear and convincing evidence that the ends of
        justice and the best interest of the public as well as the defendant or
        the arrested person will be served by and order of expungement.. [S.D.
        Codified Laws § 23A-27-17 (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - An application or petition has been submitted and accepted. [S.D.
        Codified Laws §§ 24-14-1 et seq. (2013)]
      - Approval or recommendation from the board of pardons. [S.D. Codified
        Laws §§ 24-14-1 et seq. (2013)]
      - Notice has been sent to the prosecuting attorney, or their successor,
        the sentencing judge, the attorney general, and the law enforcement
        agency where the offense was committed, no later than thirty days prior
        to the pardon hearing. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-3 (2013)]
      - The applicant must publish for three consecutive weeks in a
        county-designated newspaper notice of the applicant's hearing, along
        with their name, the offense, time of conviction, and term of
        imprisonment. The final notice must be published no later than twenty
        days prior to the pardon hearing. (This notice requirement does not
        apply to exceptional pardons.). [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-4 (2013)]
      - Any person feeling aggrieved by the pardon application has been given
        the chance to testify against the pardon. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-6
        (2013)]
      - After five years from the date of their release from imprisonment, or,
        in cases where no prison sentence was served the date of the offense,
        applicants convicted of not more than one non-life felony may apply for
        an exceptional pardon. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-8 (2013)]
      - After a petition has been denied by the Governor, the Board may not
        accept a repeat petition for executive clemency for the same person
        until one full year has elapsed from the date of the denial.. [S.D. DoC
        Pardon FAQs page]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - No. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-11 (2013),S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-26
        (2013)]

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Individuals convicted of a crime of violence or certain felony drug
        offense lose their right to possess a firearm for 15 years from the date
        of their discharge from prison, jail, probation, or parole. Individuals
        convicted of any other felony drug offense lose the right to possess a
        firearm for 5 years from the date of completion of their sentence..
        [S.D. Codified Laws § 22-14-15 (2013),S.D. Codified Laws § 22-14-15.1
        (2013)]
      - Individuals convicted of a misdemeanor domestic violence offense lose
        their right to own a firearm for 1 year from the date of conviction.
        [S.D. Codified Laws § 22-14-15.2 (2013)]

        [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-27(2) (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - Although not technically resulting in a conviction, a defendant who has
        successfully completed their probation term as part of a suspended
        imposition of sentence, the court shall then discharge and dismiss the
        matter without an adjudication of guilt, and thereafter order the
        sealing of all public records related to the matter.. [S.D. Codified
        Laws § 23-6-8.1 (2013)]
      - For non-public records maintained by the Bureau of Criminal Statistics,
        the director of the Bureau may authorize the destruction of records
        under the following circumstances: (i) the person has died; (ii) the
        person has attained the age of 75 years old and has not violated any
        laws in the previous 10 years; (iii) the incident involves offenses no
        longer considered crimes in the state; or (iv) misdemeanor offenses
        disposed of at least 10 years prior.. [S.D. Codified Laws § 23A-3-30
        (2013)]
      - The court has found by clear and convincing evidence that the ends of
        justice and the best interest of the public as well as the defendant or
        the arrested person will be served by and order of expungement.. [S.D.
        Codified Laws § 23A-27-17 (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - An application or petition has been submitted and accepted. [S.D.
        Codified Laws §§ 24-14-1 et seq. (2013)]
      - Approval or recommendation from the board of pardons. [S.D. Codified
        Laws §§ 24-14-1 et seq. (2013)]
      - Notice has been sent to the prosecuting attorney, or their successor,
        the sentencing judge, the attorney general, and the law enforcement
        agency where the offense was committed, no later than thirty days prior
        to the pardon hearing. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-3 (2013)]
      - The applicant must publish for three consecutive weeks in a
        county-designated newspaper notice of the applicant's hearing, along
        with their name, the offense, time of conviction, and term of
        imprisonment. The final notice must be published no later than twenty
        days prior to the pardon hearing. (This notice requirement does not
        apply to exceptional pardons.). [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-4 (2013)]
      - Any person feeling aggrieved by the pardon application has been given
        the chance to testify against the pardon. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-6
        (2013)]
      - After five years from the date of their release from imprisonment, or,
        in cases where no prison sentence was served the date of the offense,
        applicants convicted of not more than one non-life felony may apply for
        an exceptional pardon. [S.D. Codified Laws § 24-14-8 (2013)]
      - After a petition has been denied by the Governor, the Board may not
        accept a repeat petition for executive clemency for the same person
        until one full year has elapsed from the date of the denial.. [S.D. DoC
        Pardon FAQs page]
