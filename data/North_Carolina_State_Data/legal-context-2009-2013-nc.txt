Legal Context - North Carolina

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302 (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - Infractions. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302(b) (2013)]
      - All misdemeanors. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302(b) (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - None. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302 (2013)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302 (2013)]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - Any law enforcement officer. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302 (2013)]
      - Other authorized personnel. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-302 (2013)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana has not been decriminalized. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        90-95(d)(4) (2013),N.C. Gen. Stat. § 90-94(1) (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner has never had a criminal record expunged. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-146]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner has never been convicted of a felony. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - All charges where expunction is sought occurred within the same 12-month
        period. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures.
        [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner was acquitted at trial on all charges where expunction is
        sought. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were dismissed. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-146]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 13. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7B-2200 (2013)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7B-1501(17) (2013)]

Cases Rejected for Prosecution

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner has never had a criminal record expunged. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-146]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner has never been convicted of a felony. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - All charges where expunction is sought occurred within the same 12-month
        period. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures.
        [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner was acquitted at trial on all charges where expunction is
        sought. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were dismissed. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-146]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - A petition to expunge is filed in the court where the petitioner was
        convicted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - At least 15 years have passed since the date of conviction or since any
        active sentence has been served (including periods of probation or
        post-release supervision). [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to a summary conviction. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner has never been convicted of a felony or misdemeanor other
        than a traffic violation. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to a single conviction for either a
        nonviolent felony or a nonviolent misdemeanor. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to multiple nonviolent felony or
        nonviolent misdemeanor convictions that occurred in the same session of
        court, and none of the offenses are alleged to have occurred after the
        individual has been served with criminal process. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures.
        [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner has no outstanding fines, interest, or restitution
        orders. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - An application or petition has been submitted and accepted. [N.C. Const.
        art. III, § 5, cl. 6]
      - Approval from the governor. [N.C. Const. art. III, § 5, cl. 6]
      - The offense for which clemency is sought is not a conviction resulting
        from impeachment. [N.C. Const. art. III, § 5, cl. 6]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - No. [N.C.G.S. §15A-153(a)]

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of a felony until all
        periods of incarceration, probation, and parole are completed. [N.C.G.S.
        §13-4(1)]
      - Firearm restrictions. [N.C.G.S. §14-404(c)(1), (c)(2), (c)(3), (c)(5)]
      - Denial of the opportunity to adopt. [N.C.G.S. §48-3-309]
      - Removal of child custody rights. [N.C.G.S. §50B-2]
      - Six month ban from the food and nutrition services program after release
        from custody for certain drug related felonies. [N.C.G.S. §108A-25.2]
      - Six month ban from the Work First Program after release from custody for
        certain drug-related offenses. [N.C.G.S. §108A-25.2]
      - Termination of parental rights. [N.C.G.S. §7B-1111]
      - Deportation. [N.C.G.S. §15A-1022]

Nonviolent Misdemeanor Cases with Nonmonetary Release (ROR)

  * Pretrial Release Eligibility

    * Offenses presumptively denied or ineligible for pretrial release:

      - Capital offenses.
      - Certain drug trafficking offenses. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-533(d) (2013)]
      - Certain Gang-related offenses. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-533(e) (2013)]
      - Manufacturing of methamphetamine or possession of an immediate precursor
        chemical. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534.6 (2013)]
      - Certain offenses involving the illegal use, possession, or discharge of
        a firearm. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-533(f) (2013)]
      - Terrorism. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534.5 (2013)]
      - If a defendant is alleged to have committed an offense while still
        residing in or during an unauthorized absence from involuntary
        commitment in a mental health facility, they do not have a right to
        pretrial release when charged with any capital or noncapital offense.
        [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-533(a) (2013)]
      - Violation of certain health control measures if person poses health and
        safety threat. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534.5 (2013)]
      - Violation of post-release supervision or parole (for a maximum of 7 days
        if no preliminary hearing on parole violation or revocation hearing is
        held). [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1368.6(b) (2013),N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-1368.6(b) (2013)]
      - Any offense for which the defendant has a pending felony charge or is
        subject to sex offender registration if danger to public (for a maximum
        of 7 days unless a judicial official makes a determination that the
        defendant poses a danger to the public). [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1345(b1)
        (2013)]
      - Any offense for which the defendant is an out-of-state probationer
        arrested for violation of probation if subject to Interstate Compact for
        Adult Supervision. [Ch. 148, Art. 4B]
      - Any offense for which the defendant is found to be a fugitive from
        another state charged with offense punishable by life in prison or
        death, or fugitive charged with any offense after arrest on Governor’s
        warrant. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-736 (2013)]

    * Does state law require the use of a risk assessment tool in deciding
      pretrial release?

      - No. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534(c) (2013)]

      - Restrictions on contact with certain associates. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-534(a)(5) (2013)]
      - Restrictions on place of abode. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534(a)(5) (2013)]
      - Restrictions on possession and use of alcohol and/or controlled
        substances. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534(a)(5) (2013)]
      - Home detention. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534(a)(5) (2013)]
      - Electronic monitoring. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-534(a)(5) (2013)]
      - Participation in a 24/7 drug and/or alcohol monitoring program. [N.C.

      - Submitting to a fingerprint or DNA sample. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-534(a)(5) (2013)]
      - Any conditions necessary to ensure the defendant's appearance in court

Pretrial Diversion of Nonviolent Misdemeanors

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Judges. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1) (2013)]
      - Prosecutors. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1) (2013)]
      - Defendants. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1) (2013)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Probation without verdict. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1) - (a5)
        (2013),N.C. Gen. Stat. § 14-204 (2013)]
      - Substance abuse treatment programs. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7A-793
        (2013),N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a2) (2013)]
      - Alcohol abuse treatment programs. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7A-793 (2013)]
      - Drug court. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7A-793 (2013)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants with no prior convictions. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 14-204(b)(1)
        (2013)]
      - Defendants identified as alcohol abusers. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-1341(a2), (a5) (2013)]
      - Defendants not currently participating in a diversion program. [N.C.
        Gen. Stat. § 14-204(b)(1) (2013)]
      - Defendants who have not previously entered into a diversion agreement.
        [N.C. Gen. Stat. §15A-1341(a1), (a4) (2013)]
      - Defendants who voluntarily agree to participation. [N.C. Gen. Stat.
        §15A-1341(a1), (a4) (2013)]
      - Defendants not previously convicted of certain possession offenses.
        [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 90-96(a) (2013)]
      - Defendants eligible for community or intermediate punishment. [N.C. Gen.
        Stat. § 15A-1341(a) (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine the eligibility of defendants,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7A-797 (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - All misdemeanors. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1),  (a4) (2013)]
      - Class H and I felonies. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1),  (a4) (2013)]
      - Offenses eligible for intermediate punishment. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-1341(a) (2013)]
      - Offenses eligible for community punishment. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-1341(a) (2013)]
      - Certain offenses related to prostitution. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        15A-1341(a3) (2013),N.C. Gen. Stat. § 14-204 (2013)]
      - Certain offenses related to the use or possession of drugs and
        paraphernalia. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a2), (a5) (2013),N.C. Gen.
        Stat. § 90-96(a) (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [N.C. Gen. Stat.
        § 7A-797 (2013)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Any offense involving moral turpitude. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1341(a1),
        (a4) (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [N.C. Gen. Stat.
        § 7A-797 (2013)]

        (2013)]
      - Criminal extradition hearings. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7A-451(a) (2013)]
      - Cases involving involuntary commitment. [N.C. Gen. Stat. § 7A-451(a)
        (2013)]
      - Proceedings for the provision of protective services. [N.C. Gen. Stat. §
        7A-451(a) (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification

      - The petitioner has never been convicted of a felony. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]

        period. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures.
        [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The petitioner was acquitted at trial on all charges where expunction is
        sought. [N.C.G.S. §15A-146]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were dismissed. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-146]

      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - A petition to expunge is filed in the court where the petitioner was
        convicted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]

        active sentence has been served (including periods of probation or
        post-release supervision). [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to a summary conviction. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner has never been convicted of a felony or misdemeanor other
        than a traffic violation. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to a single conviction for either a
        nonviolent felony or a nonviolent misdemeanor. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to multiple nonviolent felony or
        nonviolent misdemeanor convictions that occurred in the same session of
        court, and none of the offenses are alleged to have occurred after the
        individual has been served with criminal process. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures.
        [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner has no outstanding fines, interest, or restitution
        orders. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]

  * Sentencing Systems

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - A petition to expunge is filed in the court where the petitioner was
        convicted. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - At least 15 years have passed since the date of conviction or since any
        active sentence has been served (including periods of probation or
        post-release supervision). [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to a summary conviction. [N.C.G.S.
        §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner has never been convicted of a felony or misdemeanor other
        than a traffic violation. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to a single conviction for either a
        nonviolent felony or a nonviolent misdemeanor. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The record in question pertains to multiple nonviolent felony or
        nonviolent misdemeanor convictions that occurred in the same session of
        court, and none of the offenses are alleged to have occurred after the
        individual has been served with criminal process. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures.
        [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]
      - The petitioner has no outstanding fines, interest, or restitution
        orders. [N.C.G.S. §15A-145.5]

      - The offense for which clemency is sought is not a conviction resulting
