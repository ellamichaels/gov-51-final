  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - Please note, the law(s) governing expungement have changed since the
        time of this study period. Please refer to the current statutes for most
        up-to-date information.. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140 (2019)]
      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        610.140(1) (2013)]
      - The court determines that the arrest was based on false information.
        [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122 (2013)]
      - There is no probable cause to believe the petitioner committed an
        offense. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(1) (2013)]
      - The petitioner did not face any charges as a result of the arrest. [Mo.
        Rev. Stat. § 610.122(2) (2013)]
      - The petitioner has no prior or subsequent felony or misdemeanor
        convictions. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(3) (2013)]
      - The petitioner did not receive a suspended imposition of sentence for
        any offense related to the arrest. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(4) (2013)]
      - No civil action is pending related to the arrest or the records sought
        to be expunged. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(5) (2013)]

Cases Rejected for Prosecution

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - Please note, the law(s) governing expungement have changed since the
        time of this study period. Please refer to the current statutes for most
        up-to-date information.. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140 (2019)]
      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        610.140(1) (2013)]
      - The court determines that the arrest was based on false information.
        [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122 (2013)]
      - There is no probable cause to believe the petitioner committed an
        offense. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(1) (2013)]
      - The petitioner did not face any charges as a result of the arrest. [Mo.
        Rev. Stat. § 610.122(2) (2013)]
      - The petitioner has no prior or subsequent felony or misdemeanor
        convictions. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(3) (2013)]
      - The petitioner did not receive a suspended imposition of sentence for
        any offense related to the arrest. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(4) (2013)]
      - No civil action is pending related to the arrest or the records sought
        to be expunged. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.122(5) (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - Please note, the law(s) governing expungement have changed since the
        time of this study period. Please refer to the current statutes for the
        most up-to-date information.. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140 (2019)]
      - A petition to expunge is filed in the court where the petitioner was
        convicted. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(1) (2013)]
      - The conviction is a felony or misdemeanor offense for passing a bad
        check, fraudulently stopping payment of an instrument, or fraudulently
        using a credit device or debit device. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(2)(1)
        (2013)]
      - The conviction meets a specified list of offenses eligible to be
        expunged which includes only select misdemeanor offenses, excluding the
        felony offenses listed above. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(2)(2),(3)
        (2013)]
      - At least 20 years have passed, including any period of incarceration,
        probation, or parole, if the offense was a felony. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        610.140(5)(1) (2013)]
      - At least 10 years have passed, including any period of incarceration,
        probation, or parole, if the offense was a misdemeanor, municipal
        offense, or infraction. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(5)(1) (2013)]
      - The petitioner has paid any amount of restitution ordered by the court.
        [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(5)(3) (2013)]
      - The expungement is consistent with public welfare, determined in the
        court hearing. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(5)(5) (2013)]
      - The petitioner has not received a previous order of expungement from the
        same court. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(10) (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - An application or petition has been submitted and accepted. [Mo. Rev.
        Stat. § 217.800(2) (2013)]
      - Approval from the governor. [M.O. Const. art. IV, § 7]
      - The offense for which the pardon is being sought is not treason, nor is
        it a case of impeachment. [M.O. Const. art. IV, § 7]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - No. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.120(2) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(7)
        (2013)]

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of a felony until all
        periods of incarceration, probation, and parole are completed. [Mo. Rev.
        Stat. § 561.026(1) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. § 115.133(2) (2013)]
      - Loss of voting rights for individuals currently incarcerated for a
        misdemeanor. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 561.026(1) (2013)]
      - Permanent loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of any offense
        connected with the exercise of the right of suffrage. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        561.026(2) (2013)]
      - Permanent loss of right to serve as a juror for individuals convicted of
        a felony. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 561.026(3) (2013)]
      - Loss of right to own or possess a firearm for individuals convicted of a
        felony. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 571.070(1)(1) (2013)]
      - Individuals who have a felony conviction for child abuse or neglect,
        spousal abuse, a crime against children, or a crime involving violence,
        including rape, sexual assault, or homicide, but not including other
        physical assault or battery shall not be eligible to receive an adoption
        or guardianship subsidy. [Mo. Code Regs. tit. 13, § 35-38.010(4)(A)]
      - Any individual who in the past five years has had a felony conviction
        for physical assault, battery, or a drug-related offense shall not be
        eligible to receive an adoption or guardianship subsidy. [Mo. Code Regs.
        tit. 13, § 35-38.010(4)(B)]
      - Individuals convicted of sexual offenses, offenses against the family,
        or pornography and related offenses when the victim was a child may
        immediately lose their custody rights. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 210.117(1)
        (2013)]
      - Individuals convicted of abetting, attempting, or conspiring to commit,
        or committed murder of another child of the parent may have their
        parental rights terminated. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 211.447(2)(3)(a),(c)
        (2013)]
      - Individuals convicted of committing a felony assault that resulted in
        serious bodily injury to the child or to another child of the parent may
        have their parental rights terminated. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        211.447(2)(3)(d) (2013)]
      - Individuals found guilty or pled guilty to a felony sexual offense or
        incest when the victim was their child may have their parental rights
        terminated. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 211.447(5)(4) (2013)]

    * Offenses presumptively denied or ineligible for pretrial release:

      - Capital offenses. [M.O. Const. art. I, § 20]
      - Any offense committed by a person reasonably presumed to be an alien
        unlawfully present in the United States. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 544.470(2)
        (2013)]

    * Does state law require the use of a risk assessment tool in deciding
      pretrial release?

      - No. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 544.455 (2013)]

Pretrial Diversion of Nonviolent Misdemeanors

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Judges. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.008(4)(3) (2013)]
      - Prosecutors. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.005(1) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        478.1000(2) (2013)]
      - All pretrial diversion decisions shall be upon agreement of the involved
        parties, including the defendant and defense attorney. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        478.008(4)(3) (2013)]
      - Circuit courts are authorized to establish conditions for referral. [Mo.
        Rev. Stat. § 478.008(3) (2013)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Drug court. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.001 (2013)]
      - DUI court. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.007 (2013)]
      - Veterans programs. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.008 (2013)]
      - Criminal nonsupport court. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.1000 (2013)]
      - Community corrections program. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 217.777 (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to implement any pretrial diversion program,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 217.777 (2013)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants who voluntarily agree to participation. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        478.008(4)(3)(a) (2013)]
      - Defendants identified as nonviolent persons. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        478.005(1) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.1000(2) (2013)]
      - Military veterans or current military personnel suffering from substance
        abuse or mental illness. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.008(1),(2) (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Cases which stem from substance abuse or mental illness for military
        veterans or current military personnel. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.008(1)
        (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        478.008(3)(1) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.1000(2) (2013)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        478.008(3)(1) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. § 478.1000(2) (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - Please note, the law(s) governing expungement have changed since the
        time of this study period. Please refer to the current statutes for the
        most up-to-date information.. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140 (2019)]
      - A petition to expunge is filed in the court where the petitioner was
        convicted. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(1) (2013)]
      - The conviction is a felony or misdemeanor offense for passing a bad
        check, fraudulently stopping payment of an instrument, or fraudulently
        using a credit device or debit device. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(2)(1)

        expunged which includes only select misdemeanor offenses, excluding the
        felony offenses listed above. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 610.140(2)(2),(3)

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of a felony until all
        periods of incarceration, probation, and parole are completed. [Mo. Rev.
        Stat. § 561.026(1) (2013),Mo. Rev. Stat. § 115.133(2) (2013)]
      - Loss of voting rights for individuals currently incarcerated for a
        misdemeanor. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 561.026(1) (2013)]
      - Permanent loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of any offense
        connected with the exercise of the right of suffrage. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        561.026(2) (2013)]
      - Permanent loss of right to serve as a juror for individuals convicted of
        a felony. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 561.026(3) (2013)]
      - Loss of right to own or possess a firearm for individuals convicted of a
        felony. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 571.070(1)(1) (2013)]
      - Individuals who have a felony conviction for child abuse or neglect,
        spousal abuse, a crime against children, or a crime involving violence,
        including rape, sexual assault, or homicide, but not including other
        physical assault or battery shall not be eligible to receive an adoption
        or guardianship subsidy. [Mo. Code Regs. tit. 13, § 35-38.010(4)(A)]
      - Any individual who in the past five years has had a felony conviction
        for physical assault, battery, or a drug-related offense shall not be
        eligible to receive an adoption or guardianship subsidy. [Mo. Code Regs.
        tit. 13, § 35-38.010(4)(B)]
      - Individuals convicted of sexual offenses, offenses against the family,
        or pornography and related offenses when the victim was a child may
        immediately lose their custody rights. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 210.117(1)
        (2013)]
      - Individuals convicted of abetting, attempting, or conspiring to commit,
        or committed murder of another child of the parent may have their
        parental rights terminated. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 211.447(2)(3)(a),(c)
        (2013)]
      - Individuals convicted of committing a felony assault that resulted in
        serious bodily injury to the child or to another child of the parent may
        have their parental rights terminated. [Mo. Rev. Stat. §
        211.447(2)(3)(d) (2013)]
      - Individuals found guilty or pled guilty to a felony sexual offense or
        incest when the victim was their child may have their parental rights
        terminated. [Mo. Rev. Stat. § 211.447(5)(4) (2013)]
