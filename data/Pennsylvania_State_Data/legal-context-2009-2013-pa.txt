Legal Context - Pennsylvania

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [234 Pa. Code § 519(B)(1),234 Pa. Code § 441(B)]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - Summary offenses. [234 Pa. Code § 402]
      - 2nd degree misdemeanors. [234 Pa. Code § 519(B)(1)(a)]
      - 1st degree misdemeanor DUI offenses. [234 Pa. Code § 519(B)(1)(a)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - Arrestee poses a danger to self or others. [234 Pa. Code § 519(b)(1)]
      - There is reasonable suspicion that the arrestee will fail to appear in
        court. [234 Pa. Code § 519(b)(1)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - Yes. [234 Pa. Code § 519]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - The arresting officer. [234 Pa. Code § 519(B)(1)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana is illegal. [P.L. 233 No. 64, §13(a)(31)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. §
        9122 (2012)]
      - The court has ordered or approved an expunction request. [18 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 9122 (2012)]
      - The state police central repository has not received a disposition for
        the case. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122 (2012)]
      - The state police central repository did not record a disposition for the
        case within 18 months after the arrest, and the court confirms that no
        action is pending. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122 (2012)]
      - The petitioner is at least 70 years old and has not been arrested or
        faced prosecution for at least 10 years following his or her final
        release from custody. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122 (2012)]
      - The individual has been deceased for at least 3 years. [18 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 9122 (2012)]
      - The petitioner has not been placed in an Accelerated Rehabilitative
        Disposition program for sex offenses against an individual under 18. [18
        Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122 (2012)]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures. [18
        Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122 (2012)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 6355(a)(1) (2012)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 6302 (2012)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. §
        9122(a)(3),(b) (2012)]
      - The record in question pertains to a summary conviction. [18 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 9122(a)(3),(b) (2012)]
      - The petitioner is at least 70 years old and has not been arrested or
        faced prosecution for at least 10 years following his or her final
        release from custody. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122(a)(3),(b) (2012)]
      - The individual has been deceased for at least 3 years. [18 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 9122(a)(3),(b) (2012)]
      - The petitioner has been free of arrest or prosecution for five years
        following the conviction for which expunction is sought. [18 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 9122(a)(3),(b) (2012)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - An application or petition has been submitted and accepted. [P.A. Const.
        art. IV,  § 9]
      - Approval from the governor. [P.A. Const. art. IV,  § 9]
      - Majority approval from the board of pardons. [P.A. Const. art. IV,  § 9]
      - Unanimous approval from the board of pardons if a death sentence has
        been imposed and clemency counsel is sought. [P.A. Const. art. IV,  § 9]
      - The offense for which clemency is sought is not a conviction resulting
        from impeachment. [P.A. Const. art. IV,  § 9]
      - In the case of a sentence of death or life incarceration, a full hearing
        in open session must be held following due public notice. [P.A. Const.
        art. IV,  § 9]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - Yes. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 9122(e) (2012)]

  * Other collateral consequences

      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Ineligibility for TANF, the General Assistance program, food assistance,
        and medical assistance for individuals convicted of fraudulently
        applying to these programs. [55 Pa.Code § 141.21(s)]
      - Loss of voting rights for individuals currently incarcerated for a
        felony. [Voting Rights of Convicted Felons, Convicted Misdemeanants, and
        Pretrial Detainees]
      - Firearm restrictions. [18 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 6105(a)(1),(b) (2012)]
      - Denial of the opportunity to adopt. [23 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 6344(p)(2)
        (2012)]
      - Removal of child custody rights. [23 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 5329(a) (2012)]
      - Termination of parental rights. [23 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 2511(a)(7),(9)
        (2012)]
      - Loss of veterans benefits. [43 Pa.Code § 7.11(2)]
      - Deportation. [61 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 6143 (2012)]

Pretrial Diversion of Nonviolent Misdemeanors

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Judges. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 1520(a) (2012),35 Pa. Cons. Stat. §
        780-117(1)]
      - Magistrates. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 1520(a) (2012)]
      - Counties are authorized to refer and admit candidates to diversion
        programs. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 916 (2012),75 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1552
        (2012)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Probation without verdict. [35 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 780-117]
      - Community service, control, or supervision programs. [42 Pa. Cons Stat.
        § 1520(b) (2012)]
      - Vocational programs. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 1520(b) (2012)]
      - All problem solving courts. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 916(a) (2012)]
      - Education programs. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 1520(b) (2012)]
      - Accelerated rehabilitative disposition. [75 Pa. Cons Stat. § 1552
        (2012)]
      - Drug court. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 916(a) (2012)]
      - Mental health court. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 916(a) (2012)]
      - DUI court. [35 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 780-117]
      - Counties are authorized to implement any pretrial diversion program,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [42 Pa. Cons Stat. § 916(a) (2012)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants with no prior convictions. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1520 (2012)]
      - Defendants who have not previously entered into a diversion agreement.
        [35 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 780-117(1)]
      - Defendants who voluntarily agree to participation. [35 Pa. Cons. Stat. §
        780-117(1)]
      - Defendants not charged with multiple, separate, and unrelated offenses.
        [35 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 780-117(1)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine the eligibility of defendants,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 916 (2012),75
        Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1552 (2012)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Summary offenses or infractions. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1520(a) (2012)]
      - Any nonviolent offense. [35 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 780-117]
      - Certain offenses related to DUIs. [75 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1552 (2012)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [42 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 916(a),(b) (2012)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Offenses related to motor vehicles. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1520(a)
        (2012)]
      - Offenses related to hunting or game. [42 Pa. Cons. Stat. § 1520(a)
        (2012)]
      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [42 Pa. Cons.
        Stat. § 916(a) (2012)]
