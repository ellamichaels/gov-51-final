Legal Context - Arkansas

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [Ark. R. Crim. P. 5.2]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - All misdemeanors. [Ark. R. Crim. P. 5.2]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - When law enforcement has probable cause to believe a violation of a
        post-conviction no contact order has taken place. [Ark. Code §
        5-4-106(f)(2) (2015)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [Ark. R. Crim. P. 5.2]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - Any law enforcement officer or their superior. [Ark. R. Crim. P. 5.2]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana is illegal. [Ark. Code § 5-64-419(b)(5)
        (2015),Ark. Code § 5-64-215(a)(1) (2015)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ark. Code §
        16-90-1409 (2015)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the arrest, if no accusatory instrument
        was filed. [Ark. Code § 16-90-1409 (2015)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [Ark. Code § 9-27-318(c)(2) (2015)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [Ark. Code § 9-27-303(32)(A) (2015)]

Cases Rejected for Prosecution

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ark. Code §
        16-90-1409 (2015)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the arrest, if no accusatory instrument
        was filed. [Ark. Code § 16-90-1409 (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ark. Code §
        16-90-1405 (2015)]
      - The petitioner has completed all requirements of the sentence, including
        any financial obligations. [Ark. Code § 16-90-1405(a) (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - In all criminal cases, except those of treason and impeachment, the
        Governor shall have the power to grant pardons. [Ark. Const. art. VI, §
        18]
      - In cases of treason, the Governor shall have the power to grant pardons
        only after Senate approval. [Ark. Const. art. VI, § 18]
      - In cases of capital murder, or of a Class Y, A, or B felony, a copy of
        the application for pardon or commutation shall be filed with, the
        Secretary of State; the Attorney General; the sheriff of the county
        where the offense was committed; the prosecuting attorney of the
        judicial district where the applicant was found guilty and sentenced, if
        still in office, and, if not, the successor of that prosecuting
        attorney; the circuit judge presiding over the proceedings at which the
        applicant was found guilty and sentenced, if still in office, and, if
        not, the successor of that circuit judge; and the victim of the crime or
        the victim's next of kin, if he or she files a request for notice with
        the prosecuting attorney. [Ark. Code § 16-93-204(d)(2) (2015),Ark. Code
        § 5-4-607(b)(1) (2015)]
      - Application has been referred to the Parole Board for investigation.
        [Ark. Code § 16-93-204(a)(3) (2015)]
      - The Parole Board has had an opportunity to investigate the applicant’s
        case, has made a pardon recommendation to the Governor, and has made a
        public notice of their recommendation. [Ark. Code § 16-93-204 (2015)]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - No. [Ark. Code § 16-90-1416 (2015)]

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of a felony. [Ark.
        Const. art. III, § 2]
      - Loss of right to own or possess a firearm for individuals convicted of a
        felony. [Ark. Code § 5-73-103(a)(1) (2015)]
      - Loss of adoption rights if convicted of a felony offense relating to
        child abuse or neglect, spousal abuse, a crime against children, or a
        crime involving violence, including rape, sexual assault, or homicide,
        but not including other physical assault or battery. [Ark. Code §
        9-28-116(c)(1)-(4) (2015)]
      - Loss of adoption rights if convicted of a felony offense relating to
        aggravated assault on a family or household member. [Ark. Code §
        9-28-116(c)(5) (2015)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [Ark. Code § 9-27-318(c)(2) (2015)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [Ark. Code § 9-27-303(32)(A) (2015)]

Pretrial Diversion of Nonviolent Misdemeanors

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - All pretrial diversion decisions shall be upon agreement of the involved
        parties, including the defendant and defense attorney. [Ark. Code §
        16-98-201 (2015)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Substance abuse treatment programs. [Ark. Code § 16-98-201 (2015)]
      - Drug court. [Ark. Code § 16-98-303 (2015)]
      - Pre-adjudication probation program. [Ark. Code § 5-4-903 (2015)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Individuals whose authorized sentences for the crimes they are charged
        with include eligibility for suspended sentence. [Ark. Code §
        16-98-201(2) (2015)]
      - Individuals charged with a felony offense. [2# - Ark. Code § 5-4-904
        (2015)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - All felonies. [9 - Ark. Code § 5-4-904 (2015)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - An offense for which the person would be required to register as a sex
        offender. [ALL - Ark. Code § 5-4-904(b)(2) (2015)]
      - Violent felony as defined by statute. [ALL - Ark. Code § 5-4-904(b)(2)
        (2015)]
      - A felony involving a victim who was seventeen years of age or younger at
        the time the felony was committed. [ALL - Ark. Code § 5-4-904(b)(2)
        (2015)]
      - A felony involving a victim who was sixty-five (65) years of age or
        older at the time the felony was committed. [ALL - Ark. Code §
        5-4-904(b)(2) (2015)]

    * Does state law allow pretrial diversion programs to charge a fee to
      defendants for admission and participation?

      - Yes. [1 - Ark. Code § 5-4-907(a)(5) (2015)]

    * Does state law require that jurisdictions have a pretrial services office?

  * Sentencing Systems

    * Type of sentencing system:

      - Structured and indeterminate. [Making Sense of Sentencing: State Systems
        and Policies]

    * According to an assessment conducted by the National Center of State
      Courts, to what extent are the state's sentencing guidelines mandatory?

      - Arkansas scores a 4 out of 12, where larger scores indicate more
        mandatory guidelines.. [NCSC State Sentencing Guidelines]

    * Taking into consideration the level of compulsion mandated by the state's
      sentencing guidelines, are judges compelled to provide written rationale
      for any sentences departing from the guidelines?

      - Yes; departures for plea deals require written rationale, however, that
        is not the case for bench trials. [NCSC State Sentencing Guidelines]

      - Class Y felony offenses. [Ark. Code § 5-4-401(a)(1)-(4) (2015)]

        the prosecuting attorney. [Ark. Code § 16-93-204(d)(2) (2015),Ark. Code
        § 5-4-607(b)(1) (2015)]
      - Application has been referred to the Parole Board for investigation.
        [Ark. Code § 16-93-204(a)(3) (2015)]
      - The Parole Board has had an opportunity to investigate the applicant’s
        case, has made a pardon recommendation to the Governor, and has made a
        public notice of their recommendation. [Ark. Code § 16-93-204 (2015)]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - No. [Ark. Code § 16-90-1416 (2015)]

      driver's license (suspension or revocation may be either mandatory or
      discretionary):

      - Failure to pay any fines as ordered by the court for a conviction of
        littering (mandatory). [Ark. Code § 8-6-406(b)(3) (2015)]
      - Refusal to submit to a chemical test (mandatory). [Ark. Code §
        5-65-205(b) (2015)]

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - Loss of voting rights for individuals convicted of a felony. [Ark.
        Const. art. III, § 2]
      - Loss of right to own or possess a firearm for individuals convicted of a
        felony. [Ark. Code § 5-73-103(a)(1) (2015)]
      - Loss of adoption rights if convicted of a felony offense relating to
        child abuse or neglect, spousal abuse, a crime against children, or a
        crime involving violence, including rape, sexual assault, or homicide,
        but not including other physical assault or battery. [Ark. Code §
        9-28-116(c)(1)-(4) (2015)]
      - Loss of adoption rights if convicted of a felony offense relating to
        aggravated assault on a family or household member. [Ark. Code §
        9-28-116(c)(5) (2015)]
