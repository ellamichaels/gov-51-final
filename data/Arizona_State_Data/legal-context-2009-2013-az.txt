Legal Context - Arizona

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3903 (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - Any misdemeanor or petty offense. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3903 (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - Citations may be issued for any misdemeanor or petty offense, however,
        if those offenses include domestic violence, sexual offenses, and
        driving under the influence require the accused to provide
        fingerprints.. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3903(C) (2013)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3903(B) (2013)]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - The arresting officer. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3903 (2013)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of marijuana is illegal. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3405 (2013)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-4051(A) (2013)]
      - Instances in which the individual has been wrongfully arrested,
        indicted, or otherwise charged, and they have been cleared of those
        charges, a judge may order a notation on the record stating as such.
        Furthermore, the order shall require all law enforcement agencies and
        courts to not release copies of or provide access to the record to any
        persons except upon order of the court.. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-4051(B)
        (2013)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-501 (2013)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 8-201(6) (2013)]

Cases Rejected for Prosecution

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-4051(A) (2013)]
      - Instances in which the individual has been wrongfully arrested,
        indicted, or otherwise charged, and they have been cleared of those
        charges, a judge may order a notation on the record stating as such.
        Furthermore, the order shall require all law enforcement agencies and
        courts to not release copies of or provide access to the record to any
        persons except upon order of the court.. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-4051(B)
        (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-907(A)(C) (2013)]
      - The relevant convicted offense is not a dangerous offense, a sex
        offense, did not involve a victim under 15 years old, and was not a
        traffic offense except for reckless driving. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-907(D) (2013)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - For felonies committed before 1994, a petition for pardon must be sent
        to the Board of Executive Clemency, who has exclusive power to pass on
        and recommend pardons to the governor, who after recommendation may
        grant pardons for all offenses except impeachment. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        31-402(A)(B)(C) (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 31-443 (2013)]

    * Do expunged records appear in public record requests?

      - Information not located in statutes.

  * Other collateral consequences

    * Other collateral consequences besides the suspension or revocation of a
      driver's license that may result from a conviction:

      - A felony conviction suspends a person's right to vote. [Ariz. Rev. Stat.
        § 13-904(A)(1) (2013)]
      - Any person who has not been previously convicted of a felony shall have
        their right to vote automatically restored following successful
        completion of their probation or absolute discharge from imprisonment
        and has paid any fines or restitution imposed. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-912(A) (2013)]
      - A felony conviction suspends the person's right to possess a gun or
        firearm. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-904(A)(5) (2013)]
      - Denial of the opportunity to adopt. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 8-105(D)
        (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 41-1758.07(B),(C) (2013)]
      - Recent drug offense or driving under the influence convictions may deem
        a person ineligible for child custody. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 25-403.04(A)
        (2013)]
      - Child custody shall not be granted to a person convicted of first degree
        murder, where the victim was the child's other parent. [Ariz. Rev. Stat.
        § 25-403.05(A)(2) (2013)]
      - A parent deprived of liberties due to a felony conviction may be deemed
        ineligible for child custody. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 8-533(B)(4) (2013)]

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 14. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-501 (2013)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 18. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 8-201(6) (2013)]

Nonviolent Misdemeanor Cases with Nonmonetary Release (ROR)

  * Pretrial Release Eligibility

    * Offenses presumptively denied or ineligible for pretrial release:

      - Capital offenses. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3961(A) (2013)]
      - Sexual assault. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3961(A) (2013)]
      - Sexual conduct with a minor who is under fifteen years of age. [Ariz.
        Rev. Stat. § 13-3961(A) (2013)]
      - Molestation of a child who is under fifteen years of age. [Ariz. Rev.
        Stat. § 13-3961(A) (2013)]
      - Any class 1, 2, 3, or 4 felony or aggravated DUI, if there is probable
        cause to believe that the defendant has entered or remained in the
        United States illegally. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3961(A) (2013)]
      - Any felony offenses committed when the person charged is already
        admitted to bail on a separate felony charge. [A.Z. Const. art. II, §
        22, cl. A]
      - Any felony offenses if the person charged poses a substantial danger to
        any other person or the community, if no conditions of release which may
        be imposed will reasonably assure the safety of the other person or the
        community and if the proof is evident or the presumption great as to the
        present charge. [A.Z. Const. art. II, § 22, cl. A]
      - Terrorism. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3961(D)(1),(2) (2013)]
      - Dangerous crimes against children. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3961(D)(1),(2)
        (2013)]

    * Does state law require the use of a risk assessment tool in deciding
      pretrial release?

      - No. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-3967(B) (2013)]

Pretrial Diversion of Nonviolent Misdemeanors

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Prosecutors. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-365 (2013)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Bad check programs. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-1810(A) (2013)]
      - Drug court. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 3422(A) (2013)]
      - Counties are authorized to implement any pretrial diversion program,
        subject to statutory restrictions. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-361
        (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-362 (2013)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants not previously convicted of a serious violent offense. [Ariz.
        Rev. Stat. § 11-361 (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-706(F) (2013)]
      - Defendants not previously convicted of a sex offense. [Ariz. Rev. Stat.
        § 11-361 (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 13-1401 (2013)]
      - Defendants not previously convicted of a dangerous offense (as defined
        by statute). [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-361 (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-105(13) (2013)]
      - Defendants not previously convicted three or more times of possession of
        a controlled substance or drug paraphernalia. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        11-361(2) (2013)]
      - Defendants not previously convicted of dangerous crimes against
        children. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-361 (2013),Ariz. Rev. Stat. §
        13-705(P)(1) (2013)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Counties are authorized to determine which offenses are eligible for
        pretrial diversion, subject to statutory restrictions. [Ariz. Rev. Stat.
        § 11-361(1) (2013)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - A previous conviction of a serious offense as defined by statute. [Ariz.
        Rev. Stat. § 11-361(1) (2013)]
      - A previous conviction of a sexual offense. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-361(1)
        (2013)]
      - A previous conviction of a dangerous crime as defined by statute. [Ariz.
        Rev. Stat. § 11-361(1) (2013)]
      - A previous conviction of a dangerous crime against children as defined
        by statute. [Ariz. Rev. Stat. § 11-361(1) (2013)]
