Legal Context - Oregon

Non-Custodial Promise to Appear Instead of Custodial Arrest

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

    * Can citations be issued as an alternative to custodial arrest for criminal
      offenses?

      - Yes. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(1) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.070
        (2015)]

    * Offenses eligible for citations instead of arrest:

      - All misdemeanors. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(2)(a) (2015)]
      - Violations of city or county ordinance. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.070
        (2015)]
      - Felony authorized to be reduced to a misdemeanor. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        133.055(2)(a) (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - Domestic violence offenses. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(2)(a) (2015)]

    * Are citations presumptive for eligible offenses?

      - No. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(1) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.070 (2015)]

    * Authorities who may issue citations:

      - Peace officer. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(1) (2015)]

  * Sentencing Options

    * Legal status of marijuana:

      - Possession of an amount less than 1 oz. is a violation. [Or. Rev. Stat.
        § 475.864(3)(c) (2015)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which an arrest record may be cleared (qualification
      requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner is not involved in any pending criminal procedures. [Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - At least 1 year has passed since the arrest, if no accusatory instrument
        was filed. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(b) (2015)]
      - The petitioner was acquitted at trial on all charges where expunction is
        sought. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(b) (2015)]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were not prosecuted. [Or. Rev.
        Stat. § 137.225(1)(b) (2015)]
      - The charges where expunction is sought were dismissed. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(1)(b) (2015)]
      - A copy of the motion to clear the record has been sent to the
        prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The prosecutor has been given an opportunity to contest the motion to
        clear the record. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints have been sent to the prosecutor’s office.
        [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints shall be sent to the state police, who will
        pull information resulting from a search of the fingerprints and present
        that information to the prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The victims have been notified of the motion to clear the record. [Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(b) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(c),(d) (2015)]
      - The victim has been allowed to make a statement at the hearing on the
        matter. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(3) (2015)]

  * Age & Charging

    * Minimum age at which an individual can be prosecuted as an adult:

      - 15. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.707(1)(a) (2015)]

    * Age at which an individual can no longer be considered a juvenile:

      - 25. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 419C.005(4)(d) (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner is at least 70 years old and has not been arrested or
        faced prosecution for at least 10 years following his or her final
        release from custody. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - At least 3 years has passed since the pronouncement of judgment. [Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - In instances where a person whose sentence of probation was revoked, a
        period of at least 10 years has passed from the date of probation
        revocation. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(c) (2015)]
      - A copy of the motion to clear the record has been sent to the
        prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The prosecutor has been given an opportunity to contest the motion to
        clear the record. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints have been sent to the prosecutor’s office.
        [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints shall be sent to the state police, who will
        pull information resulting from a search of the fingerprints and present
        that information to the prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The victims have been notified of the motion to clear the record. [Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(b) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(c),(d) (2015)]
      - The victim has been allowed to make a statement at the hearing on the
        matter. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(3) (2015)]
      - The conviction was for an offense other than class A felony or other
        ineligible offense. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(5),(6) (2015)]
      - The individual has successfully completed their diversion plan and a
        post-treatment period of three years, provided there have been no new
        convictions for misdemeanor or felony offenses. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        430.505 (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which pardons may be granted:

      - An application or petition has been submitted and accepted. [Or. Rev.
        Stat. § 144.650 (2015)]
      - Approval from the governor. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 144.649 (2015)]
      - The convicted offense for which the pardon is sought is not treason.
        [Or. Const. art. V, § 14]

Pretrial Diversion of Nonviolent Misdemeanors

  * Pretrial Diversion

    * Individuals responsible for pretrial diversion admission decisions (may be
      program-specific):

      - Prosecutors. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(1) (2015)]

    * Types of pretrial diversion authorized by state law:

      - Substance abuse treatment programs. [Or. Rev. Stat. §§ 430.450 et. seq.
        (2015)]
      - Bad check programs. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.925 (2015)]
      - Deferred prosecution. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(1) (2015)]
      - DUII diversion program. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 813.200 (2015)]

    * Types of defendants eligible for pretrial diversion (program eligibility
      may be satisfied by different combinations of criteria):

      - Defendants admitting to use of, or addiction to, drugs. [Or. Rev. Stat.
        § 430.485 (2015)]
      - Individuals charged with negotiating a bad check. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        135.925 (2015)]
      - Individuals charged with DUI. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 813.200 (2015)]
      - District attorneys are authorized to adopt policies and guidelines
        delineating eligibility criteria for pretrial diversion. [Or. Rev. Stat.
        § 135.886(1) (2015)]

    * Offenses eligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Any nonviolent offense. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 430.455 (2015)]
      - Certain offenses related to DUIs. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 813.200(1)(2015)]
      - Offenses of forgery and counterfeiting related to writing bad checks.
        [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.925(3) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 165.065 (2015)]

    * Offenses ineligible for pretrial diversion:

      - Offenses involving serious physical injury to another person. [Or. Rev.
        Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015)]
      - Class A or B felony and involved physical injury to another person. [Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015)]
      - Rape in the first degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015),Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 163.375 (2015)]
      - Rape in the second degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015),Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 163.365 (2015)]
      - Sodomy in the first degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015),Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 163.405 (2015)]
      - Sodomy in the second degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015),Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 163.395 (2015)]
      - Unlawful sexual penetration in the first degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        135.886(2)(3) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 163.411 (2015)]
      - Unlawful sexual penetration in the second degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        135.886(2)(3) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 163.408 (2015)]
      - Sexual abuse in the first degree. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3)
        (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 163.427 (2015)]
      - Offenses involving domestic violence, at the time the offense was
        committed, the defendant was subject to a protective order in favor of
        the victim of the offense. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 135.886(2)(3) (2015)]

    * Does state law allow pretrial diversion programs to charge a fee to
      defendants for admission and participation?

      - Yes. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 813.210(2) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 813.240(3)
        (2015)]

        was filed. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(b) (2015)]

        sought. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(b) (2015)]

        137.225(1)(b) (2015)]

        prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]

        clear the record. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints have been sent to the prosecutor’s office.
        [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]

        pull information resulting from a search of the fingerprints and present
        that information to the prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(2)(a) (2015)]

        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(b) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(c),(d) (2015)]
      - The victim has been allowed to make a statement at the hearing on the
        matter. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(3) (2015)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared

      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(1)(a) (2015)]

        release from custody. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(a) (2015)]

      - In instances where a person whose sentence of probation was revoked, a
        period of at least 10 years has passed from the date of probation
        revocation. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(c) (2015)]
      - A copy of the motion to clear the record has been sent to the
        prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The prosecutor has been given an opportunity to contest the motion to
        clear the record. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints have been sent to the prosecutor’s office.
        [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints shall be sent to the state police, who will
        pull information resulting from a search of the fingerprints and present
        that information to the prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(2)(a) (2015)]

        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(b) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(c),(d) (2015)]
      - The victim has been allowed to make a statement at the hearing on the
        matter. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(3) (2015)]
      - The conviction was for an offense other than class A felony or other
        ineligible offense. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(5),(6) (2015)]

        post-treatment period of three years, provided there have been no new
        convictions for misdemeanor or felony offenses. [Or. Rev. Stat. §

Jail Capacity Utilization

  * Citation in Lieu of Arrest

      offenses?

      - Yes. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(1) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.070
        (2015)]

      - All misdemeanors. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(2)(a) (2015)]
      - Violations of city or county ordinance. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.070
        (2015)]
      - Felony authorized to be reduced to a misdemeanor. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        133.055(2)(a) (2015)]

    * Circumstances under which citations shall not be issued:

      - Domestic violence offenses. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(2)(a) (2015)]

      - No. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.055(1) (2015),Or. Rev. Stat. § 133.070 (2015)]

  * Clearing a Criminal Record

    * Circumstances under which a conviction record may be cleared
      (qualification requirements may be satisfied by different combinations of
      the criteria):

      - A petition or request has been filed and accepted. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - The petitioner is at least 70 years old and has not been arrested or
        faced prosecution for at least 10 years following his or her final
        release from custody. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(a) (2015)]
      - At least 3 years has passed since the pronouncement of judgment. [Or.

      - In instances where a person whose sentence of probation was revoked, a
        period of at least 10 years has passed from the date of probation
        revocation. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(1)(c) (2015)]
      - A copy of the motion to clear the record has been sent to the
        prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The prosecutor has been given an opportunity to contest the motion to
        clear the record. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints have been sent to the prosecutor’s office.
        [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The defendant’s fingerprints shall be sent to the state police, who will
        pull information resulting from a search of the fingerprints and present
        that information to the prosecutor’s office. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        137.225(2)(a) (2015)]
      - The victims have been notified of the motion to clear the record. [Or.
        Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(b) (2015)]
      - The petitioner pays a fee. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(2)(c),(d) (2015)]
      - The victim has been allowed to make a statement at the hearing on the
        matter. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(3) (2015)]
      - The conviction was for an offense other than class A felony or other
        ineligible offense. [Or. Rev. Stat. § 137.225(5),(6) (2015)]
      - The individual has successfully completed their diversion plan and a
        post-treatment period of three years, provided there have been no new
        convictions for misdemeanor or felony offenses. [Or. Rev. Stat. §
        430.505 (2015)]
